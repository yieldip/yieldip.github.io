7 Reasons to Register a Trade Mark

Registering a trade mark is one of the most strategic decisions a brand owner can make. Here are seven compelling reasons why securing your mark should be a priority.

1. Registered trade mark as a sword

A trade mark registration allows you as the brand owner to stop others from using the same or similar mark that potentially causes consumer confusion.

It gives you the exclusive right to use the trade mark, as well as to authorize other persons to use the trade mark, in relation to the goods and/or services for which the trade mark is registered.

2. Registered trade mark as a shield

Owning trade mark rights over your brands also prevent others from usurping or freeriding on your goodwill, and from damaging your reputation by associating your brand with an inferior product.

3. Registered trade mark offers a partial freedom-to-operate

Pursuant to Section 28(3) of the Trade Marks Act, "a registered trade mark is not infringed by the use of another registered trade mark in relation to goods or services for which the latter is registered". This means that you cannot be alleged of infringing an earlier registered mark existing on the Singapore register, even if your trade mark is successfully registered later. A trade mark registration in Singapore therefore accords a partial freedom-to-operate.

4. Streamlined infringement action is possible

This point is related to using your trade mark as a sword. Obtaining a trade mark registration would grant you a statutory monopoly. This means that if someone else uses the same/similar mark on the same/similar goods and/or services, then you can rely on your registration as proof of your right to the mark and sue the other party for trade mark infringement.

The "infringement" process is more streamlined compared to the common law action of "passing off" which may pose some additional hurdles due to the requirement of proving reputation and goodwill.

5. Perpetual protection is possible

Trade mark registrations can be renewed every 10 years, enabling perpetual protection so long as the rights are duly renewed.

This is unlike other intellectual property rights such as patents (lasts a total of 20 years only, subject to payment of annuities yearly), registered designs (lasts a total of 15 years only, subject to payment of renewal fees every 5 years) and copyright (duration depends on the category of copyright works).

Important: Such perpetual rights remain subject to actual use of your trade mark in association with the goods and/or services it is registered for. Under Section 22 of the Trade Marks Act, any person may apply to revoke a registered trade mark on either of the following grounds:

  1. The mark has not been put to genuine use by the proprietor or with his consent for a five-year period following completion of the registration procedure and there are no proper reasons for non-use; or
  2. Genuine use has been suspended for an uninterrupted period of five years and there are no proper reasons for non-use.

It is therefore pertinent to ensure that your trade mark is used in the form that it is registered, to avoid removal of the mark from the Register on the basis of non-use and continue to reap the benefits of perpetual protection.

6. Commercialization of trade mark rights

Registered trade marks can also be licensed for commercial use by other third parties for negotiated royalties, thereby creating another stream of revenue. This is often done via licensing or franchising agreements.

7. Registered trade marks create and strengthen brand value

Trade marks create brand value — they potentially help you build and improve your brand image and identity, functioning as distinctive badges of origin of your goods and services, while also assuring consumers of the high quality of your products and services. They work in tandem with other marketing efforts, to distinguish and set you apart from your competitors.

Ready to protect your brand?

Registering a trade mark comes with many benefits and they are valuable assets to companies. It is prudent to engage the services of IP professionals to assist you.
