Be Your Own Dentist — Yay or Nay?

As a trademark attorney with over 10 years of experience, I often see business owners tempted by the ease of online trademark filing. While technology has made the process more accessible, handling it entirely on your own can lead to significant pitfalls — much like attempting your own dental work.

True success requires more than just submission. Your mark must meet strict legal standards: it needs to be distinctive (acting as a unique identifier for your goods or services), non-descriptive, and free from conflicts with existing trademarks on the Singapore register.

The Hidden Risks of Going Solo

Without expert guidance, common errors can derail your application. A thorough conflict search — conducted by a professional — is essential to assess your mark's registrability and spot identical or confusingly similar earlier marks. This step is especially critical for international expansion, as it gauges your approval odds and prevents downstream issues.

Objections from IPOS alone can cost SGD 500–2,000 or more to resolve, depending on their scope and complexity. Oppositions during the two-month publication period are far costlier, often exceeding SGD 20,000–30,000 due to counter-statements, evidence filing, hearings, and negotiations.

Why Goods/Services Matter

Selecting the right goods and services is another minefield. Broad or poorly drafted descriptions risk overlapping with competitors, inviting objections or oppositions. Pre-approved IPOS lists help, but a professional ensures your specifications align precisely with your business while minimising exposure.

The Value of Professional Strategy

Expertise in crafting a tailored filing strategy pays for itself by avoiding these expensive hurdles. As a specialist, I conduct comprehensive searches, refine your specifications, and position your mark for smooth registration — locally and globally.
